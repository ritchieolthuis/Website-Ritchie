Vitens, Grootzakelijke markt: Market Needs, Positioning & Organisational Direction

For Vitens, I conducted an in-depth market study to determine how a new Water-as-a-Service division should be positioned, structured, and brought to market. The research examined which parties in the Netherlands offer similar services, the differences in propositions, and how Vitens can distinguish itself through circular water solutions and water stewardship. I analysed target segments, assessed customer needs, and identified which services should (and should not yet) be included in the WaaS portfolio. The study also explored naming, branding and organisational form, and evaluated how Vitens can bridge from its current service boundary to a commercial WaaS entity that contributes to a more resilient and sustainable water system. These insights provide strategic support for Vitens in shaping the future of its large business segment.

Perron038, Factory NEXT: Strategic Commercial Positioning

At Perron038, I explored how Factory NEXT can be commercially positioned and made attractive for industrial partners. The research focused on determining the market potential for a shared innovation facility, identifying which companies and segments would adopt a “test-before-invest” model, and analysing competing initiatives in the Dutch manufacturing landscape. I evaluated how Factory NEXT’s labs, technologies and expertise could be translated into clear value propositions and developed strategic recommendations on messaging, target segments, and revenue models. By combining internal and external analysis with the Design Thinking cycle and customer validation, I delivered insights that support Perron038 in strengthening Factory NEXT as a commercially viable and distinctive innovation hub.
